</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>1916 -</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>At the request of the Inspector of Schools, the parish priest of the Church
of the Nativity of the Blessed Virgin Mary, Father H. Duvelle, started
a class to teach English and Chinese to some of the children of the parish.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>1920 -</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>Rev. Father E. Becheras succeeded Rev. Father H. Duvelle who reorganized
the school into an English school with a class of 30 pupils under the charge
of Mr Monterio. The school was initially known as Holy Innocents’ English
School. An additional class was formed the following year.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>1922 -</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>The school expanded steadily until it had 9 classrooms. M.P.A.D Costa
became the first lay principal of the school.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>1936 -</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>The management of the school was placed in the hands of the Brothers of
St Gabriel. Rev. Bro Gerard Majella came from Bangkok to become the first
Brother Director of the school.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>1946 -</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>Rev. Brother Gerard Majella was succeeded by Rev. Brother Louis.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>1948 -</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>The school was extended by having a floor added to the main block. An
“afternoon” school was inaugurated. Enrolment increased to 1200.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>1955 -</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>Rev. Brother Noel became principal of the school. He set up a Building
Fund Committee to raise funds for the extension of the school.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>1959 -</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>Rev Brother Albert was principal of the school.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>1960 -</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>Holy Innocents’ English School was renamed Montfort School in honour of
the Founder, St Louis Marie De Montfort, of the Gabrielite Brothers.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>1961 -</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>The school opened its doors to female students. They were admitted to
the Pre-University classes which catered to both the Science and Art students.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>1973 -</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>The last batch of Pre-U students graduated from the school. Students would
have to register at Junior Colleges or Pre-U classes from the following
year.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>1974 -</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>The big split took place. Montfort School which had been functioning as
a full school would henceforth function separately as Montfort Junior School
and Montfort Secondary School. Rev Brother Albert was principal of the
Secondary school and Mr Wee Bak Hoe was principal of the Junior school.
Enrolment stood at about 900 pupils in the Secondary school and 1600 in
the Junior school.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>1987 -</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>A Building Fund Committee was set up to raise funds for the new Montfort
School at the present site at Hougang Avenue 8.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>1989 -</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">

new Principal of Montfort Junior.</p>
</td>
</tr>
